Projects

HackDFW
In spring 2015, a group of friends and I won HackDFW, a student hackathon in Dallas. We created a virtual ecosystem of dinosaurs whose behavior evolved over time. There is some information about the project on ChallengePost here
Java tree storage
Over the summer of 2014, I wrote a Java program to organize data for a mentorship program at my high school. About 100 students were organized into trees where each student had at least one mentor and was a mentor to at least one student. My program drew out the trees of mentors and allowed the user to retrieve information about the students (such as contact information to give to the student’s mentor).
Android Apps
I used Processing, a language based on Java, to write two Android apps that can be found on the Google Play Store.
  1. Boids Tank : This implements the flocking algorithm ‘boids’ developed by Craig Reynolds to simulate schooling fish. The fish follow each other around the screen and change color based on the colors of the fish around them
  2. Drop Nine : I wrote a version of a game that I like where the player has to clear the screen of small balls. It is similar to tetris
Pirate Game
In 2014, my senior year of high school, I went to HackBCA, a high school hackathon in New Jersey, and made a platformer called Yarr! with my team. After the hackathon, I rewrote the majority of the code and remade the graphics. It can be found here
